A quick history


The history of Mt. Rainier ranges very widely, with some early volcanic deposits, being found formed from the lily formation, which happened as late as 2.9 million years ago. However, the version of Mt. Rainier that we see today is about 500,000 years old, as it was made on top of the older Mt. Rainier remains. Although it is hard to tell how many times Mt.Rainier has actually erupted, it is known that the last time eruptive activity wa reorded was in 1894-95.

Natural Life

Many prominent species of the Pacific Northwest also inhabit Mt. Rainier national park, including the Black bear, Cougar, bobcat and redfox. Marine species, mostly fish, also inhabit this environment, with the majority of them being byll trout, cutthroat troat and mountain whitefish. Anandromous or migratory fish, also appear in the park and special time periods. Last of all, the flora of Mt. Rainier National park is plentiful and diverse as well. There are over 960 vascular(plants with vascular tissue) plant species and more than 260 nonvascular plant species that have been identified.

Preservation

Currently the main problem many National Park service volunteers are facing in Mt. Rainier National Park, is the spreading of invasive species. The main plants that Volunteers are combating are the common Dandelion and the Yellow Hawkweed. Rivers must be especially monitored, as a threat present in them is a threat present to every animal in the park.
