A quick history

The Olympic Mountains we know and love today began to formover 35 million years ago when the farallon plate collided with the North American Plate. However the Mountains oly came above ea level about 10-20 million years ago. Some of their biggest changes happened during the Pleistocene era, when multiple retreating glaciers carved, the narrow valleys that shape the National Park today.

Nature

The Olympic National Park holds the uniqueness of having one of the most diverse ecosystems in the entire world, sporting a luxury of beaches, mountains, rainforests, and rivers. Some large parts of the Olympic National are known for having unbroken sretches of wilderness, with no Human contact in sight

Tourism

Although a few roads reach into the mountain park, they do not go far into the interior, meaning that it will usually take a couple days to get to the interior of the National Park as you will be using hiking trails. Although most people tour the National park over the summer, the Winter offers many recreational activities as well such as skiing and sledding. Boating, Rafting and Fishing are all activities you can enjoy in bodies of water, along with swimming sometimes as well

Preservation

The Olympic National Park is famous for having the second largest ecosystem resoration project in the history of the National Parks service after the Everglades National Park. It is called the Elwha Ecosystem Restoration Project, named after the Elwha River. The majority of the project consisted of removing two dams on the Elwha river, and is considered the argest Dam removal in history!
